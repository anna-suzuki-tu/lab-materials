LAB OS — CHAPTER 04

The 5-Layer Structure

Strategic organization of lab activities

Lab activities are designed in five layers

Each layer has its own role. Don't mix them.

  1. Lab Forum — Value refinement space
  2. Team Meeting — Tactical decision space
  3. Student Interaction — Autonomy training space
  4. Individual Meeting — Identity alignment space
  5. Individual Work — The only place research moves

Layers 1–4: four meetings, four roles

1. Lab Forum — where we polish the Why

Refine value and positioning. Constructive doubt and philosophical deep dives from diverse perspectives. Lab Forum is for sparring, not for conclusions.

2. Team Meeting — decisions that move research forward

Paper Card → decision → next action. GO / NO-GO. Move to the next phase, or go back. Team Meeting is for decisions, not for sparring.

3. Student Interaction — autonomy training

Technical consultation, verbalization training, pre-review. Practice explaining your work to peers before bringing it to Team Meeting or the supervisor.

4. Individual Meeting — confirming your research axis

Growth direction and career design. Not for technical details or minor manuscript edits — those belong in Team Meeting or Student Interaction.

Decisions vs. sparring

Team Meeting = DecisionLab Forum = Sparring
Decides GO / NO-GO
Move to the next phase, or go back		 Break and polish questions, positions, expressions
Not a place for conclusions

Decisions move the phases; sparring polishes them.

Layer 5: Research doesn't move in meetings

Research doesn't move in meetings. It moves at your desk.

Individual Work is where research actually progresses. Required daily habits at Layer 5:

  • Daily — Research Log (what you did) + Thinking Log (what you thought)
  • Weekly — Paper Card update (keep updating the claim)
  • Monthly — Core Question check

Meeting is for alignment. Work is for progress.

Core Principle — four places, four purposes

PlacePurpose
Team MeetingTactics — Tactical Decision
Lab ForumValue — Value Refinement
Individual MeetingAxis — Identity Alignment
Individual WorkProgress

Always know what each place is for. Never mix the purposes — that is how the five layers run.
