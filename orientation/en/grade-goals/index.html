LAB OS — CHAPTER 05

Grade-Specific Goals

The more freedom you have, the more responsibility you carry

The more freedom, the more responsibility

Two axes define your position in the lab: research responsibility (horizontal) and community responsibility (vertical). As you move from B4 → Master → PhD, you move toward the upper right — more independence, and more accountability for the lab's direction and culture.

Responsibilities by grade — B4 / Master / PhD

B4: Experience & Honesty

Run one full research cycle. Share results and struggles honestly. Completion over perfection.

Master: Completion & Mentorship

Submit one Original article. Support and guide the B4s. The responsibility to finish the race.

PhD: Definition & Culture

Define a unique research axis. Protect and evolve the lab culture. A Review paper is one way to establish the axis.

GradeBaseline expectation
B41 full research cycle — experience it once
Master1 Original paper — submit one first-author paper
PhDAxis + Culture — define the axis, carry the culture

These are baselines, not stretch goals. Grow along both axes — research and community.

Quarter Plan: structural visualization, not a progress report

Strategy designed in 90-day quarters. The one-year research arc split into Q1–Q4.

What it isWhy 90 days
A visualization of your thinking structure
Designs the arc before running it		 Research is a marathon; design in sprints
Short spans expose structural drift early

Strategy is quarterly — run the marathon on sprint-sized designs.

Locate yourself before moving

YOU ARE HERE. Identity Snapshot — forward progress cannot be designed from a vague location.

Before designing any plan, fix your current position: What is your Core Question today? What phase are you in? What external events are coming?

The B4 year: back-calculate from the deadlines

Not a year of research only — external events squeeze research time significantly.

  1. Q1 Apr–Jun — Fix the theme, in parallel with coursework
  2. Q2 Jul–Sep — Framing — entrance exams and job hunting squeeze research
  3. Q3 Oct–Dec — Figures — finalize the figures
  4. Q4 Jan–Feb — Thesis wrap-up: submit the thesis — one full research cycle completed

APR → FEB: design backwards from the external events.

B4 failure patterns — what the Quarter Plan prevents

  1. Stalled by graduate entrance exams
  2. Silence when stuck
  3. Waiting for "perfection" to share
  4. Total collapse from multi-tasking

Evaluation — what is actually assessed

Evaluation is a by-product of structural thinking. The criteria:

  1. Understanding
  2. Logical presentation
  3. Q&A performance
  4. Document structure

Complete the arc → skills will follow.

The reality of M2: time disappears suddenly — SEP draft → NOV mid-term → JAN submission. That is why you design in M1.

The PhD's three years: build, expand, integrate

  1. D1: Build the axis — Axis construction, Review paper (externalize the hypothesis)
  2. D2: Expand — Original expansion, deepen methods, gain independence
  3. D3: Integrate — Integration & positioning, structural design of the thesis
"Axis-less PhDs will drift."
