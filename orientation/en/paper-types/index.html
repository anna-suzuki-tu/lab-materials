LAB OS — CHAPTER 03

Paper Types & Responsibilities

Belief takes multiple forms

Paper types are not ranks

Paper types are not ranks. They differ only in scope of belief.

Original / Review / Technical / Short — equal standing, no hierarchy. The same "belief" expressed at different zoom levels.

Four basic formats

FormatWhat it fixes
OriginalA new claim — the full inquiry cycle in one paper
ReviewReframing the questions — synthesizes the field to redesign the question landscape
TechnicalMethodological — fixes a method, tool, or dataset
ShortA limited claim — fast, focused, scoped

Formats are tools, not status.

Venues of Publication

One axis is speed, the other is rigor.

VenueRole
ConferenceStart a discussion. Speed first.
JournalRigorous verification. Rigor first.
ThesisIntegrated accountability — the full arc in one document.

Different venues, different roles — use them accordingly.

A thesis vs. a journal paper

A thesis is internal accountability. A journal is external verification.

Inquiry completes in public space. The journey from Lab to Scientific Community requires external verification — a journal. A thesis alone keeps the belief inside the lab. Publishing it exposes it to the update mechanism of the broader field.

Research phase determines paper type

Choose the format that matches where you are in the research cycle:

  1. Exploration — fix small pieces with Short / Technical
  2. Stability — put out new claims with Original
  3. Establishment — redesign the questions with a Review

A PhD holds an axis — a Review is one form of it.

What is a thesis?

A thesis is not a page count. It is a coherent belief system.

Paper 1 → Paper 2 → Paper 3, connected into a Research Arc. Each paper extends or sharpens the axis; together they form an integrated argument.

Don't choose by format — choose from your question

The map-like order:

  1. Question — hold the map
  2. Format — pick the format that fits the question
  3. Venue — decide the venue last

Question → Format → Venue — reverse the order and you get lost.

Ethics is the qualification for submitting belief

A paper is an ethically submitted, verifiable belief — Claim, Ethics, Verify. Three pillars of research ethics:

  • Data Integrity — honest data and reproducibility
  • Attribution — credit and citation; show whose contribution it is
  • Transparency — disclose in a verifiable form

Ethics is covered in depth in the Writing Papers series.

Chapter 03 summary: Paper types differ in scope, not rank ／ Four formats (Original / Review / Technical / Short) ／ Three venues (Conference / Journal / Thesis) ／ Choose by question, not format ／ A thesis is a coherent belief system ／ Ethics = the qualification for submitting belief.
