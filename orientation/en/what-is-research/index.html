LAB OS — CHAPTER 01

What is Research?

Inquiry — not answer collection

Inquiry is never "finished" — it keeps being updated

Research is not a project with a deadline that wraps up with a final answer. It is a living, iterative cycle. The goal is never a terminal point — it is a sharper, better-framed question. There is no linear path: Research ≠ linear path.

The distinction matters immediately: if you think of research as collecting answers, you will feel finished the moment data arrives. If you understand it as refining questions, data becomes the beginning of the next inquiry.

Research refines questions, not answers

✕ Collecting answers○ Refining questions
Piling up answers — "Answer Pile"Sharpening the question — Refined Question
Aiming for a final answerToward a sharper, better-framed question

The goal is not a final answer — it is a sharper question.

Inquiry turns doubt into belief

  1. Doubt — "Something feels off…"
  2. Inquiry Cycle — Question → Hypothesis → Test, again and again
  3. Belief — Probabilistic, provisional, updatable

The Inquiry Cycle keeps turning: ↺. Each turn produces a more refined belief, not a finished answer.

The Inquiry Cycle: updating is the essence

Every inquiry cycle starts from a sense that "Theory ≠ Observation" — something does not add up. Follow the five steps:

  1. Misalignment — A sense that something is off
  2. Question — Frame the misalignment precisely
  3. Hypothesis — Commit to a guess
  4. Test — Iterate
  5. Belief — Provisional — always open to revision

Provisional Belief — the best estimate at this moment, and nothing more. New evidence rewrites it.

Mismatch is the beginning

The gap is the starting point. There are two kinds of generative gap:

  • Observation ≠ Understanding — the gap between what we observe and what we can explain generates questions
  • Ideal ≠ Reality — the gap between how things should work and how they do generates questions

GAP → Inquiry starts here. Don't look for a gap-free starting point. The gap itself is the entry point.

Cognition and Inquiry share one structure

The brain works the same way research does:

  1. Prediction — The brain continuously predicts the world
  2. Error — It detects the mismatch with observation
  3. Update — It updates itself to reduce the error
The brain strives to minimize prediction error. Free Energy Principle (intuitive version) — large error → discomfort → action & update ／ small error → stable belief. (K. Friston)

Belief is not "Truth" — it is Probability

  • Always provisional — the best estimate at this moment, nothing more
  • Always updatable — new evidence rewrites it
  • Strength varies with evidence — a probability distribution over hypothesis space

Hold the most probable belief — and keep updating it.

A paper is a medium that fixes questions and beliefs

A research paper is not a "final answer." It is a snapshot of the best belief at this moment.

  1. Doubt → a sense that something is off
  2. Inquiry → the cycle turns
  3. Paper → Fixed Belief: "This is our current best belief."

Once fixed in writing, peers can challenge, test, and verify — then the next inquiry cycle opens. Each paper opens new inquiry cycles.

Inquiry is submitting provisional belief for public update

Research never ends — it keeps updating. The paper is a medium to receive Feedback & Error from the scientific community. This is what makes inquiry a responsibility, not just a personal project.

Entering the laboratory means taking on that responsibility. Blaming luck for your results ends today.

Chapter 01 summary: Research = Inquiry, not answer collection ／ The Inquiry Cycle turns doubt into provisional belief ／ Gaps (mismatch) are the starting point ／ Cognition and inquiry share one structure ／ A paper fixes the belief so others can update it.
