ACT 1
Your Greatest Enemy Is Perfectionism
Write

Once Figures Are Fixed, Just Write

At the Figures stage of your Paper Card, the figure set is frozen. Once that happens — write the next day. The order is fixed:

🌱 Idea → 🧭 Framing → 🖼 Figures → ✍️ Draft

The Draft stage has one rule: write it all the way through, rough is fine. Struggling with a sentence is a signal that the logic or claim is still unclear. Struggling is not a sign of poor English — it is a marker of where the argument still needs work.

Struggling IS a marker of novelty — it means you are articulating something that has not been said before.

Multi-Stage Drafting: Dump → Structure → Revise

Do not try to write a clean draft in one pass. Use three stages:

1
Dump — Write everything out without editing. Facts, claims, half-formed thoughts. No self-censoring.Goal: get something onto the page. A blank page is the enemy.
2
Structure — Read what you dumped and impose order. Does each sentence have a reason for being there? Can you say why every sentence is in its position?A passage that doesn't land signals a flaw in the logic or the claim.
3
Revise — Polish sentence by sentence. Cut redundancy. Sharpen the claim.Your own stumbling is a preview of the reviewer's stumbling. Fix what trips you.
Paper Card can be the stem of your writing — your contributions are already written as "We show that …"

Sharpening the Manuscript Until It Can Withstand Peer Review

1
A methods paper lives or dies by the strength of its claim"We made X possible for the first time," not merely "we observed X."
2
Read the output sentence by sentenceIt won't get stronger until you can say, for every sentence, why it's there.
3
A passage that doesn't land signals a flaw in the logic or the claimDon't dismiss unreadability as style — your own snag previews the reviewer's snag.
4
The smell of AI erodes a reviewer's trustRemoving it is a defense for getting through review, not an aesthetic preference.
5
Anticipate the review — don't just react to itOwn weaknesses as stated limitations. AI is a drafting tool, not the author.
ACT 2
A Paper Isn't Finished With the Manuscript Alone
Polish

Authorship Needs All Four Criteria, Not Some

The ICMJE defines authorship by four pillars. All four are required — not some.

1
Substantial contribution to conception, design, data acquisition, or analysis
2
Drafting or critically revising the manuscript
3
Final approval of the version to be published
4
Accountability for all aspects of the work

"Just collected data" or "just funded it" → those contributions go in Acknowledgements, not on the author list.

All four criteria, or it isn't authorship. Decide author order before you start writing — disputes at submission are the most draining. Talk early, not at the last moment. When unsure, ask Suzuki.

Acknowledgements Record Sub-authorship Contributions Accurately

Acknowledgements need consent and accuracy too.

Data Is Now Reviewed as Part of the Paper

Open science is the default. Many journals have data-sharing policies, and data is now held to the same standard as the paper itself.

  • FAIR: Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable
  • Use a DOI-backed repository (e.g., Zenodo)
  • README must include: file naming, variable definitions, ORCID, software requirements, dates in ISO 8601 (YYYY-MM-DD)
  • State the license explicitly (CC0 / CC-BY)
Your data is a package addressed to a stranger. Package it so a stranger can use it without asking you.

Even Your Own Text Can Become Self-Plagiarism

Text recycling has section-specific rules. Extra caution is required when turning a thesis into a journal paper.

SectionReuseConditions
Methods○Reuse is common, but cite the source
Introduction△Wholesale copying is not allowed
Results / Discussion✕No reuse (counts as duplicate publication)
How much reuse is allowed depends on the section.

AI Cannot Be an Author

What AI cannot do AI cannot sign a copyright agreement or bear ethical responsibility. Authors bear full responsibility for hallucinated (fake) citations generated by AI.
How to use AI responsibly If AI was used, disclose: tool name, version, and purpose in Methods or Acknowledgements. Check each publisher's policy. Feeding an unpublished manuscript to AI = breach of confidentiality and copyright.
What can't be held accountable can't be an author. Suzuki Lab practice: log every AI tool use and check the disclosure statement before submission.

The Cover Letter Is Your 90-Second Pitch to the Editor

1
Most desk rejections are decided right hereThe cover letter is the editor's first and sometimes only impression of your work.
2
A copy-pasted abstract is spotted instantlyThe cover letter must be written, not recycled.
3
Say why this journal, why now, and what's newIn your own words — not the words of your abstract.
The cover letter is a 90-second first impression. A copy-pasted abstract signals that you don't understand the journal.

Choosing Your Journal: Four Axes

Scope fitDoes this journal publish this type of work?
Impact levelIs your contribution strong enough for this tier?
SpeedHow long is the typical review process?
Open accessFunder requirements? Article processing charges?
Read the scope statement. Read recent papers in the journal. Ask Suzuki before submitting.

Submission Checklist — Suzuki Lab

  • Core Question is answered explicitly in Discussion
  • Introduction and Discussion mirror each other
  • Every figure is referenced in the text and carries a 1-sentence claim
  • Author list and order confirmed with Suzuki
  • Acknowledgements: all contributors listed with consent, grant numbers exact
  • Data sharing: repository, README, and license in place
  • AI usage logged and disclosure statement written
  • Cover letter written (not a copy-paste of the abstract)
  • Journal scope confirmed: re-read 3 recent papers in the journal
ACT 4
A Reviewer Is Not Your Opponent
Dialogue

How Peer Review Works

Wrong frame Review = attack / Reviewer = adversary → You respond defensively → Relationship worsens → Research does not improve
Right frame Review = dialogue from a different perspective → Reviewer = your first collaborator → Blind spots surface → Research becomes stronger

The 72-hour rule: do not touch the response letter for 72 hours after receiving a decision. Let the emotional reaction settle. Then read the comments as information.

Major revision means you are still in the running. Treat it as an opportunity, not a rejection.

The Response Letter

What not to do Defensive explanations / Ignoring comments / Vague responses ("we have revised this section") / No indication of where the change appears in the manuscript
What to do Thank the reviewer specifically / Quote the comment in full / State your response clearly / Show exactly where in the manuscript the change appears / If you disagree, explain why with evidence
Every comment gets a response. "We have noted this" is not a response. Show your reasoning.

What to Do With an Unfair Review

Before escalating Re-read 72 hours later. Ask Suzuki. A comment that felt "unfair" often contains a valid point buried under the tone.
When escalation is warranted Factually incorrect claims / Out-of-scope objections / Requests that contradict the journal's scope. Document clearly and write to the editor — calmly and with evidence.
